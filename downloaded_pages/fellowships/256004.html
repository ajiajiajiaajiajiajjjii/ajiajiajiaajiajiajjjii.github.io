Descrizione sintetica in italiano L'approccio Intra-Operativo nella radioterapia (IORT) con elettroni prevede l’erogazione di una grande dose di radiazioni (15-25 Gy) sul tumore durante la chirurgia.
Descrizione sintetica in inglese The approach of Intra-Operative
electron Radio Therapy foresees the delivery of a large dose of ionizing radiation (15-25 Gy) to the tumor or tumor bed during
surgery, in order to eradicate the residual tumor cells that surgery can leave over. Long-term
cancer control and a higher survival rate has been documented in patients who had IORT as a component of their multidisciplinary
treatment. However, the amount of IOeRT procedures is quite low with respect to other radiotherapy procedures. The main
underlying reason is related to the lack of a Treatment Planning System, due to the complex challenge in performing imaging during
surgery.
CETRA (aCoustic wavEs guided inTra-operative RAdiotherapy) project aims to change such landscape, proposing an innovative tool
capable of intra-operative imaging, paving the way at online treatment planning and in-vivo dosimetry. The device will exploit the
acoustic waves induced by the beam due to the heat generation in the irradiated region.
