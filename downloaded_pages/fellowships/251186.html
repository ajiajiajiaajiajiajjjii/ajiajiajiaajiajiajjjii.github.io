Descrizione sintetica in inglese The identification of antibodies to the plasma membrane water channel Aquaporin4 (AQP4),
allows to differentiate between Neuromyelitis Optica (NMO) and other demyelinating diseases. A
small percentage of patients with NMO also presents antibodies directed against myelin
oligodendrocytic glycoprotein (MOG) in the peripheral blood. To detect the presence of NMO-IgG
antibodies in patient serum, a "Cell Based Assay" is performed, which uses cells transfected with
human AQP4 isoform M23. The development and use of an anti-MOG antibody assay would be
important. AQP4 is also expressed in skeletal muscle associated with other proteins, playing a key role in the neuromuscular system and its alteration is involved in different myopathies. Restoring the
expression of this channel in affected muscles could constitute a potential therapeutic target for the recovery of muscle activity
